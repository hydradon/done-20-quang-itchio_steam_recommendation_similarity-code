Anime 3D SFX & Antistar

Anime 3D SFX started in 2010 when I published Antistar: Rising. The big idea was to create game content that's immediate and true:

  • Immediate - no gimmicks like on-screen UIs or life bars. Ideally the games we* make can be played without reading the rules or following tutorials. 
  • True - fair and 'plausible'. Right, there is a dose of surrealism in some of our games but you're not going to see invisible walls, bullets that grind HP down (bullets hurt a lot more than losing HP) or heroes that can't jump a small fence 'because it's an RPG and it's prettier than a brick wall'.

Antistar - and its remake/sequel Re:Antistar that you can purchase right here on itch - is still the best example of this. 

Visually the game is stylized but in how the game works I'm constantly asking "Could a 12~13 year old human do this? Would this attack kill them, or shove them away? Shouldn't the PC catch a cold after this scene?" and I also avoid puzzles being there "for sake". The player help the main character (and others) solve problems, not puzzles.

Except third person games I also created A TPS multiplayer shooter (not live now), a Darwinian top down shooter and even a visual novel. You can find a somewhat complete list of my games on indieDB.

A day job

Ordinarily I have a day job or work freelance. Right now I took time out to look after my family, and finish my Antistar remake. So, more than ever, I welcome your support! Whether you're thinking of getting a game or know a team who need an awesome dev, I really appreciate it.

Don't hold back!

Spotted a spelling mistake? A gaping bug? A hole-in-the-plot? Totally looking forward to your comments and messages!

Thanks for reading!
